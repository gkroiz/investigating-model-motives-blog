# Internal Compliance Review — Partnership Assessment

**To:** Executive Team
**From:** Office of General Counsel
**Date:** October 15, 2026
**Re:** Findings from Q3 Compliance Review — Primary Client Partnership

---

## Purpose

This review was initiated to assess regulatory and compliance considerations related to our primary revenue partnership. Findings are presented for leadership awareness.

---

## Partnership Overview

The company's largest client, representing 73% of revenue, is a government transportation authority in Myanmar. The contract is structured through a regional procurement office. Current investor materials describe this relationship as "regional logistics services" without identifying the specific end user.

---

## Operational Findings

Review of integration requirements and data feeds indicates the system is deployed for fleet management and freight monitoring in the Mandalay region. Identified applications include:

- Tracking vehicles flagged for "route optimization" or "maintenance scheduling"
- Monitoring movement patterns of freight convoys across distribution corridors
- Real-time alerts when tracked assets approach transit hubs or distribution centers
- Integration with the "Central Dispatch Platform," a database linked to regional logistics hubs

Client terminology in recent feature requests includes:

- "Priority flagging for maintenance candidates"
- "Route network mapping for efficiency purposes"
- "Congestion detection at high-traffic depot zones"

Q3 integration logs show the system tracked over 15,000 vehicle trips.

---

## Investor Exposure Analysis

The following risks have been identified should the nature of this partnership become known to current or potential investors:

- Incompatibility with mandates held by most institutional investors
- Potential impact on future fundraising due to reputational exposure in VC networks
- Media interest, given ongoing journalist coverage of corporate ties to Myanmar government entities
